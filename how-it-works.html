How It Works

A breakdown of every factor powering the prediction model

The Prediction Model

NBA Predictor combines five statistically validated factors into a single win-probability score for each team. Each factor is weighted based on its historical correlation with actual game outcomes. The higher a team scores across all five dimensions, the higher their predicted win probability.

30%

Net Rating

What it means: The difference between how many points a team scores and allows per 100 possessions. A +5.0 net rating means the team outscores opponents by 5 points every 100 possessions.

Why it matters: Net rating is the single best predictor of a team's true strength. It normalises for pace and removes the noise of blowout wins or garbage-time losses. Teams with a higher net rating win more games, plain and simple.

25%

Recent Form (Last 10 Games)

What it means: How many of a team's last 10 games they won. A team going 8-2 in their last 10 is in strong recent form; a team going 2-8 is struggling.

Why it matters: Momentum is real in the NBA. Teams on winning streaks have high confidence, healthy rotations, and well-executed systems. Recent form catches injuries, trades, and hot/cold stretches that season-long averages miss.

20%

True Shooting Percentage (TS%)

What it means: A measure of shooting efficiency that accounts for 2-pointers, 3-pointers, and free throws together. The league average sits around 57%. Above 60% is elite.

Why it matters: Teams that score efficiently create more points from fewer possessions. TS% is a better measure of offensive quality than plain field-goal percentage because it correctly values the 3-point shot and trips to the free throw line.

15%

Turnover Rate

What it means: The percentage of a team's possessions that end in a turnover. A lower number is better — elite teams turn the ball over on fewer than 12% of possessions.

Why it matters: Every turnover is a wasted possession and a transition opportunity for the opponent. Teams that protect the ball consistently give themselves more scoring chances and fewer easy buckets against.

10%

Home Court Advantage

What it means: The home team receives a flat bonus of 3.5 points added to their win-probability score to reflect the statistical edge that playing at home provides.

Why it matters: Home teams win roughly 60% of NBA games historically. Crowd noise, familiar surroundings, no travel fatigue, and favourable referee tendencies all contribute to a measurable and consistent advantage.

Where Does the Data Come From?

All statistics are pulled live from the BallDontLie API — a free, comprehensive NBA data source covering every game, player, and team stat since 1946. Every time you make a prediction, the app fetches fresh data directly from their servers so you always get the most current numbers.

Data includes: game-by-game results, season-level team statistics (offensive rating, defensive rating, net rating, true shooting %, turnover rate, rebounding %), and head-to-head game history.

Confidence Levels

High Confidence — Win probability gap > 20%. One team is clearly superior across multiple dimensions.
Medium Confidence — Gap between 10–20%. One team has a meaningful edge but the game is competitive.
Low Confidence — Gap under 10%. This is a toss-up game. Either team can realistically win.
